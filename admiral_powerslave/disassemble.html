BASIC XBOX DISASSEBLY INSTRUCTIONS

  • T10 (Inside The Xbox Screws) and T15 (Case Screws) Torx screwdriver
  • Flat head screwdriver (also known as a 'slot')
Disconnect all power and A/V cords from your XBox and ensure that all devices including controllers, compact discs and / or DVD’s are removed from the system.

Turn your XBox upside down and remove all 4 screw caps (rubber feet).  Use your finger to press down on the tw0lables and run across them till you find, and make a dimple to find the other two screws.  These caps and labels cover the XBox assembly screws. (Tip: Pry the caps off gently using a small flat head screw driver.)   You can pry them off with your fingers too!!

Remove the 6 screws using the T15 Torx driver.   You can poke a hole in the labels with the driver, and unscrew them, they will break through the label, leaving it intact.  Place the screws in a safe spot.

After you have removed all 6 of the case screws, flip the XBox console right side up and then carefully remove the cover.

Remove the Power plug, and IDE (grey ribbon) cable from the XBox Hard Drive and DVD Drive.  Remove the white plug from the DVD drive, the one with the yellow wires.  Remove the HDD power cable from the guides on the plastic tray.

Use the T10 Torx driver to remove the 3 screws from the plastic trays that hold the XBox DVD Drive and the Hard Drive. Removing these will enable you to lift up both devices and expose the XBox motherboard; or, you can lift the HDD out first, then the DVD drive.  They interlock, one on top of the other.

REMOVE THE MOTHERBOARD FOR MODCHIP INSTALL.

Remove the yellow power cable, the IDE (grey ribbon) cable and the cooling fan(s) connection.

Remove the main power cable from the XBox motherboard.

Remove the XBox front panel control cable (yellow datacable for the Eject and Power buttons).

Only v1.0 Xbox: Carefully remove the XBox Controller port daughter board.  For all other XBox versions: Remove the USB datacables from the motherboard.

Using the T10 Torx driver remove the 11 screws. Once all 11 screws have been removed carefully remove the XBox motherboard from the case.

Install your mod chip, and reassemble.  Double-check all connetions and plug-ins.   Make SURE you plug the fan(s) back in!
