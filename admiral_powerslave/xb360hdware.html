The harddisk is not locked in any way. A completely zero drive will only be read by the Xbox 360 if the relevent headers are in place on the disk.

A FATX partition exists on the drive.

For a drive to be considered valid it must have the 'Plain text hard disk info' and MS logo PNG. If these elements do not exist then no HDD is detected. So there is no way for third parties to manufacture hard disks without a license or without infringing Microsoft's copyright.

Your XB360 serial number is required when formatting a HDD.

The drive's capacity is reported as 13GB by the 360 immediately after formatting.
DVD DRIVE STUFF:

There are two different rom versions 0046DH (Core) 0047DJ (Premium).  When they are swapped between each XB360 they will not boot game discs.    They may be marrying DVD drives to the Xbox as well.

The DVD drive uses the standard 7-pin Serial ATA connectors with a custom power connector.  

The 360 has a standard dual layer SATA DVD-ROM drive manufactured by Hitachi-LG - GDR-3120L.

The 360 does DVD not use the ISO9660 or UDF filesystem for the content on game DVDs.  It is supposed to be the standard xboxdvdfs, however the offset and layer breakpoint are different.

XB360 calls it \Device\CdRom0\ (found in default.xex)

Philips XBOX1 drive firmware, different dumps of the VAD6011 firmware are all identical. This means, no key or serial is present in the firmware.  However, dumps of a Toshiba/Samsung TS-H943 DVD-ROM drive. are 16 bytes are different. The difference is at offset 0x401A - 0x4029.  A 16 byte difference was also the case with the GDR-3120 dumps. 

All clues indicate that each x360 dvd-rom firmware contains an unique key. 

There doesn't appear to be a match of the key to a console id, serial, or drive serial. It's probably a unique key used in the AES routine to encrypt/decrypt communications between console and drive. Something which is new for the x360.
