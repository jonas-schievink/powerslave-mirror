The majority of the drives are not really dirty, and cleaning it will not fix the problem. What the problem is, they are getting out of adjustment or just getting weak, or the laser quit working.

1. Use a standard CD/DVD Cleaner, they look like a normal CD but with two miniature brushes on them. This will only clean the lens. Sometimes this is all you need to be up and running.

2. See: http://www.llamma.com/xbox/Repairs/xbox_dvd_repair.htm

3. Clean the lens and mirror manually.  You will have to take the DVD drive apart.  You'll see the Lense and
Mirror when you do.

4. Adjust the Potentiometer (pot) on the laser.  This adjusts the strength of the laser, and is located somewhere along the ribbon cable circuit, not on the cable itself, but from the board to the lense assembly.

5. Replace the Laser in the DVD drive, by purchasing a replacement.  This can cost the same as just buying a replacement drive on Ebay, and takes a LOT longer to do.  I wouldn't bother, but if you want the experience and satisfaction of doing it this way, be my guest.

The reason you "sometimes" get the Error, and sometimes not, is because some games (small) are on CD (Single Layer) media, and the good games are on DVD Media, and some (Large games) on DVD Dual Layer media.  The laser may be able to read the CD or maybe a Single layer DVD, but not read through to the 2nd layer of DL media.  For example: The double disk of Jet Set Radio Future and Sega GT 2003 is a CD, while Halo or Halo II are on a Dual Layer DVD.  Anything larger than 4.5GB is going to be DL Media.


Replacing the drive is the only real fix for the problem. You can open your Xbox up, remove the drive, open the drive and clean the lens and mirror. You need compressed air for the mirror.

The drives more likely to fail are the Thompson, and the Philips.
