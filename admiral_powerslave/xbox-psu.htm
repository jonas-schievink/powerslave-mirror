How to replace the Power supply or fix a burnt Fuse

First you must open your Xbox to access the PSU (Power Supply Unit)

There are two different Power supply Units available make sure if obtaining a replacement you match the motherboard connector to your box.

Use the pictures below to obtain a replacement PSU if you are just going to replace it.

  Version 1.0/1.1 Xbox's use this connector
This is like the Older PC Style Power Connectors

  Version 1.2 to 1.7 Xbox's use this connector
This is like the current PC ATX Style Power Connectors

To remove your PSU simply remove these two screws (near red arrows in photo below)

Warning!
The power supply contains capacitors which hold a charge even when the power cable is unplugged use caution when working with the PSU to avoid a shock!

1. Disconnect the PSU main power connector from the motherboard by gripping it firmly and pulling upwards, if it won't budge you may need to use a pair of pliers to help you out.

    < style="color: rgb(255, 255, 255);">2. Carefully grab the loose power cables and lift the PSU out of the Xbox while pulling upwards push through the power cable port to help release the PSU

    3. If replacing the power supply reverse the process with the new PSU and your done. If you wish to replace the fuse continue to step 4.

    <>4. flip the PSU over and find the area that looks like the photo below


    5. Carefully unsolder the fuse at the points indicated above. You may need to cut it away from some insulating white goop on the other side first.

  1. 6. Purchase a simple fuse holder such as the one in the photo below these can obtained an almost any Automotive parts store or hobby shop. Also remember to pick up some spare 5 amp fuses that fit your fuse holder assembly.


  2. 7. Now you simply have to solder the two ends of the fuse holder to the PSU circuit board by sticking the ends of the wire through the holes where the old fuse used to attach. the finished assembly should look like the photo below.

  You now have a fuse "holder" so you can replace the fuse anytime it blows out.

  If the fuse continues to blow out, then you may have another problem like an intermittent short.
