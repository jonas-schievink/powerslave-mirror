MAKING YOUR OWN CABLE:

Tools & Parts:
RJ-54 Crimper (most do RJ-11 and RJ-45), CAT-5 Cable, Male RJ-45 Connectors. The crimper should have a wire cutter, and stripper in it.

PIN OUT: [See figure 1.1]
BLUE/WHITE 1 to 3
WHITE/BLUE 2 to 6
ORANGE/WHITE 3 to 1
WHITE/ORANGE 6 to 2

BLUE/WHITE, would be the BLUE wire with the WHITE STRIPE, where as
WHITE/BLUE is a white wire with a blue stripe, the BLUE pair. You can use
any two pair combination, as long as they match pin to pin.

Strip off about a half inch of the insuation.
Fan the wires out in ORDER, make sure they are straight.
Cut with the stripper at the ends so they are all alligned up straight.
Insert the wires into the RJ-45 Connector HOOK DOWN.
Place RJ-45 into the crimper and crimp.
Repeat for the other side of the wire.

LINK CABLE:
The single link cable (for connecting two Xboxs together for Link Play) you can buy in the Bubble Pack, be it Microsoft's or Action Replay's, should also be the X-over type cable. This cable can be used to plug one end into the PC then the other into the XBOX.

Action Replay also makes a complete "Commander" four cable w/hub package. If you were to use this, you will need the hub, and wire them like this: [PC > HUB < XBOX]. The cables in this package are not Crossover cables, and can not be used to directly connect PC > XBOX or XBOX > XBOX.

Belkin makes a "Network Crossover Cable" for $25.00, which is very steep if you ask me. Saw this at Best Buy.
