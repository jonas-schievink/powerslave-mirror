NORTON GHOST- BACKUP OR UPGRADE
Connect the OEM drive to the first IDE channel as Master, and new HDD to second IDE channel as master.  This is so Ghost knows which is source and which is Target for the command line I am giving you here.

Find/Use a norton Ghost 2003 boot disk, then at the command prompt for DOS, type: "ghost /ir"

This will enable ghost to read/write in raw mode.

Clone OEM HDD to New HDD Should take 10 mins.

This should be done before you install the dashboard, or ANY other files to the Xbox's hard disk, or before upgrading the hard disk.  Create these folders on your PC's Hard Disk somewhere, and call it:  MyXbox (Example D:\MyXBox), or even C:\MyDocuments\MyXBox

Then create folders inside that folder called:
C_Drive
E_Drive
And so on.

Once your FTP client (dashboard) is loaded up, transfer (via FTP) all of the Xbox's C: to the D:\MyXBox\C_Drive folder, then Xbox's E: to D:\MyXbox\E_Drive, and so on. Xbox's E: Contains your game saves, downloaded content and audio tracks.

Can it get any simpler than that?  I DON'T THINK SO. . .

You will see F: and maybe a G: depending on the Version of EvoX Dashboard, ignore them, there is nothing in them with a STOCK HDD.  The other drives listed are temporary drives, but if it makes you feel better, create folders and transfer the contents of those drives, if there is anything in them.

Once on the PC's hard disk, you can write it to a DVD_R for safe keeping.   C: of the Xbox is the most critical.
